1. **Therapeutic effect of eravacycline against carbapenem-resistant hypervirulent Klebsiella pneumoniae in mouse models.**  
   Journal: Antimicrob Agents Chemother | Date: 2026 | Score: 10 (rule 8, llm +2)  
   Journal group: infectious_diseases_microbiology_ipc  
   Translation horizon: >12 months  
   PubMed: [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41728941/](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41728941/)  
   DOI: [https://doi.org/10.1128/aac.01237-25](https://doi.org/10.1128/aac.01237-25)  
   Type: Journal Article  
   **Trial n:** not reported  
   **Why it matters:**  
   • Carbapenem-resistant hypervirulent Klebsiella pneumoniae (CR-hvKP) presents a major clinical challenge due to its combined drug resistance and high virulence.  
   • Effective treatment options for CR-hvKP infections are severely limited, contributing to high morbidity and mortality rates.  
   • There is a critical need to identify and evaluate novel therapeutic agents capable of combating these highly resistant and virulent pathogens.  
   **Headline result:** In mouse models of CR-hvKP pneumonia, eravacycline treatment significantly reduced bacterial colonization in the lungs (P<0.01), attenuated lung injury and inflammation, and suppressed serum cytokine levels (P<0.05).  
   **Read priority:** Awareness only  
   **Clinical takeaway:**  
   • Eravacycline demonstrates promising preclinical efficacy against carbapenem-resistant hypervirulent Klebsiella pneumoniae, suggesting its potential as a future therapeutic option.  
   • These findings are from mouse models and require validation through human clinical trials before any clinical recommendations can be made.  
   • Clinicians should be aware of ongoing research into novel agents for CR-hvKP, but current practice remains unchanged.
